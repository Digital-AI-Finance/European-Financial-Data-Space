European Financial Data Space

Executive Brief for EU Policymakers
December 2025 | Version 2.0
What's at Stake: The EFDS governs financial data for 27 Member States, affecting 50,000+ reporting entities and 450 million EU citizens. Delays risk EU fintech falling behind US/Asia and biased AI making discriminatory financial decisions.

Current Status

ESAP Portal launches July 2027; full functionality 2030
FIDA First trilogue April 2025; agreement expected Q1 2026
CSRD April 2025 postponement reduces scope by 80%
DORA Effective January 17, 2025 - ICT resilience mandatory
MiCA Fully applicable December 2024; ~65% EU compliance

Critical Gaps Identified

Data Quality

  • No standards for AI training data fitness
  • 57% of executives cite ESG data quality as top challenge
  • Only 2/31 G-SIBs fully BCBS 239 compliant

Implementation

  • 69% of firms implemented <50% of PSD2
  • Collection body readiness varies by MS
  • Format selection (XML/JSON/XBRL) pending

Key Policy Debates

  • FIDA Scope: May 2025 amendments propose excluding Big Tech (DMA gatekeepers)
  • Industry Concerns: December 2024 joint statement requests impact assessment before adoption
  • SME Burden: EUR 15,000-50,000 annual compliance cost may exclude smaller players
  • Crypto-GDPR Conflict: Blockchain immutability vs. right to erasure unresolved

Economic Impact

Costs

  • FIDA (full scope): EUR 504M
  • FIDA (revised): EUR 134M
  • ESAP per MS: EUR 2-5M

Benefits

  • CMU integration: EUR 1.5T potential
  • SME cash mgmt: EUR 15-30M/bank
  • Due diligence: 30-50% reduction

Decisions Required in Next 90 Days

  1. Q1 2026: FIDA final text adoption (Council/Parliament trilogue)
  2. Q1 2026: Designate EFDS coordination leads in each Member State
  3. Q2 2026: Data format standard selection (ESAs Joint Committee)

Success Metrics

  • Access: ESAP unique users, API calls, data downloads
  • Quality: Error rates <5%, completeness >90%
  • Compliance: 27/27 collection bodies operational by July 2026
  • Innovation: 500+ new fintech providers using EFDS data by 2030
